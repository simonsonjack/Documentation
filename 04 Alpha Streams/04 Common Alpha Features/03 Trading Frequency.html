There is no hard upper or lower limit to the frequency of trading, but there are a few key considerations to keep in mind. Many funds are prop firms that prefer to trade on an intra-day or daily basis, and they might be looking for Alphas that

  • Place a few trades in the same day and end the day in cash
  • Maintain an even long/short ratio to balance out borrowing costs
  • Place trades daily or intraday and only hold positions for a few days at most
  • Re-balance weekly at most
Larger institutional investment firms might be looking for Alphas that
  • Do not trade intraday
  • Rebalance weekly or longer
  • Are long-only
  • Minimize trading frequency to keep costs low

This is not an exhaustive list, but there are a lot of practical limitations that different funds experience and investment styles to consider when building your models.
